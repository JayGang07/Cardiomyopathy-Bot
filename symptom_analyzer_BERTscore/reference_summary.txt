**PRE-CONSULTATION SUMMARY**

**Chief Complaint:**
The patient is a 55-year-old female presenting with intermittent chest pain that started a few weeks ago.

Associated symptoms are significant and include shortness of breath, dizziness, and pain that radiates to both the jaw and back. The condition moderately impacts her daily activities.

**Past Medical History:**

- **Diabetes Mellitus:** Diagnosed 5 years ago. Management appears suboptimal; the patient is not currently taking prescribed medication and monitors blood sugar less than once a month.
- **Surgical History:** History of a heart valve surgery at an unknown date for an unspecified valve. The patient does not recall further details.
- **Medications:** Currently taking Gemer DS/Geminar M2 Forte. Reports no other medications.
- **Allergies:** No known drug, food, or environmental allergies.

**Family History:**
The patient reports a positive family history for myocardial infarction, hypertension, and diabetes among close blood relatives, indicating a genetic predisposition to cardiovascular and metabolic disease.

**Lifestyle & Social History:**
- **Tobacco:** She is a current and regular smoker/vaper.
- **Alcohol:** Reports occasional use (less than once per week).
- **Diet:** Consumes a mixed diet that includes both processed and whole foods.
- **Exercise:** Engages in physical activity at least three times per week, though this is likely limited by her exertional symptoms.

**Clinical Impression:**
The patient presents with a compelling clinical picture of symptomatic ischemic heart disease. Her symptoms are highly characteristic of stable angina. Her risk profile is significantly elevated due to a combination of poorly controlled diabetes, active smoking, hypertension, and a strong family history of cardiovascular disease. The prior heart valve surgery requires further clarification. The primary concern is unstable angina or significant coronary artery disease, and urgent further diagnostic workup is warranted.
